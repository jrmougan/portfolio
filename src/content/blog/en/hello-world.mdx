## The origin

When you decide to create a portfolio as a frontend developer, the temptation is obvious: make it *pretty*. Soft gradients, glassmorphism, smooth animations, elegant typography. And that's fine. But there's a problem: **everyone does the same thing**.

## Why a terminal

The terminal aesthetic says something about you without having to write it:

- **"I know what's under the hood"** — I don't just use frameworks, I understand how things work at a low level.
- **"I value functionality"** — Content matters more than wrapping.
- **"I have my own criteria"** — I don't follow trends by inertia.

## The technical decision

I chose [Astro](https://astro.build) because it generates pure static HTML. No unnecessary JavaScript runtime. A portfolio doesn't need client-side hydration to display text.

For fonts, **Geist Pixel Square** by Vercel for headings and **Geist Mono** for body text. All self-hosted, no Google Fonts dependency.

The palette is inspired by [Dracula Theme](https://draculatheme.com/), arguably the most recognizable terminal theme in the dev ecosystem.
